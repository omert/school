Since $E$ depends only on $x$, then $p(x)=Pr(x|E)$ is either zero, if $E$
does not happen for $x$, or $Pr(x)/Pr(E)=q(x)/Pr(E)$ if it does. Hence:
  \begin{eqnarray*}
    D(p||q) &=& \sum_xp(x)\log{p(x)\over q(x)}
    \\ &=& \sum_{x\in E}p(x)\log{p(x)\over q(x)}
    \\ &=& \sum_{x\in E}{q(x)\over Pr(E)}\log{1\over Pr(E)}
    \\ &=& {-\log Pr(E) \over Pr(E)}\sum_{x\in E}q(x)
    \\ &=& {-\log Pr(E) \over Pr(E)}Pr(E)
    \\ &=& -\log Pr(E)
  \end{eqnarray*}
